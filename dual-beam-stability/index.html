Stability calculations of a two beam continuous comparison total power radiometer

Basics

Total power output of a radiometer for an input noise temperature of TTT Kelvins can be defined as

P=g⋅T P = g \cdot T P=g⋅T

where ggg is conversion factor or gain of the radiometer.

The minimum detectable noise temperature or RMS noise of a total power radiometer is

ΔTmin=TsΔt⋅B \Delta T_{\mathrm{min}} = \frac{T_{\mathrm{s}}}{\sqrt{\Delta t \cdot B}} ΔTmin​=Δt⋅B​Ts​​

where TsT_{\mathrm{s}}Ts​ is system temperature of the radiometer.

DPFU (Degrees Per Flux Unit) is a measure of the antenna performance, it is defined as

DPFU=Ae2k⋅10−26 [KJy] \mathrm{DPFU} = \frac{A_e}{2k}\cdot 10^{-26} \, \left[\frac{\mathrm{K}}{\mathrm{Jy}}\right] DPFU=2kAe​​⋅10−26[JyK​]

For example, DPFU is about 0.027 and 1.4 for Metsähovi and Effelsberg, respectively.
